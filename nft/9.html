Wheein, who has been loved by the public for her unique voice, is back with her second solo album [soar].

This album [soar], which is an extension of Wheein's first solo album [magnolia], captures the beautiful but sad image of the petals flying in the wind when the magnolia blooms and falls again. This is an attractive album where you can feel Wheein's delicate and deep musicality.

The title song <Let's Break Up> is the second song that I collaborated with emotional producer 'Junkey', and the emotion of a person who had no choice but to say goodbye after a lot of trouble was melted into a soft piano melody and Wheein's delicate yet hard vocals, which were buried deep in memory. even stimulate emotions.

The music video for <Let's Break Up>, produced as a drama tights, unfolded the story by focusing on the sad lyrics. Song Chae-yeon's delicate acting expresses the complex and subtle feelings that made her fall in love with an art teacher. Wheein's sorrowful singing appearance in the music video stimulates the viewer's emotions and makes them immerse themselves in the story even more deeply.
